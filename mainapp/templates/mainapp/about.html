 About Us 




Who We Are 

Pamoja CBO is a human centered grassroot organization that exists to improve the quality of life of communities in Western Kenya. We are passionate about helping communities develop the skills they need to make positive changes for themselves. We do so by developing, designing, supporting and cascading service based initiatives and programs to people in society who need them the most.

We prioritize health care, education, water & sanitation, youth and women economic empowerment. We have embedded real time approaches to engaging vulnerable children and adolescent women and young girls with the aim of creating happy, healthy and able communities.




Our History 

"Pamoja" (which means 'together' in Swahili) was formed in 2007 to bring together community groups and support them in achieving their objectives. Pamoja CBO developed from the observations of several people who were frequently rock climbing in Kit Mikayi area of Western Kenya. We all had backgrounds in health and development work and noticed the struggle of individuals to provide basic necessities for their families, such as safe drinking water, a healthy diet, sanitatin and education.
More importantly we noticed how many community members were working on each others' plots of land, a youth group teaching HIV prevention through a soccer club, a community run pharmacy, a widow's group which supported orphans and many other groups which had formed to bring change to the community. However, many groups worked in isolation of each other, had overlapping activities, did not have the training to maximize the impact of their efforts and lacked the resources to establish projects, which they thought were important.



Our Mission 

To empower communities to identify and address the most significant needs affecting their well-being



Our Vision 

Creating Healthy, happy and Able Communities in Kenya

Our Objectives 

 Enhance the capacity of communities to:

  • Assess their strengths, capabilities and resources
  • Assess and prioritize their needs
  • Leverage additional resources to meet their needs
  • Plan, implement and evaluate interventions

 Strengthen community leadership

 Facilitate linkages, collaboration and coordination between individuals, organizations and relevant Government Departments
